Just after midnight, the famous Orient Express is stopped in its tracks by a snowdrift. By morning, the millionaire Samuel Edward Ratchett lies dead in his compartment, stabbed a dozen times, his door locked from the inside. Without a killer among the passengers, the world's most famous detective, Hercule Poirot, must race against time to solve the case before the local police arrive.

One of Agatha Christie's most famous novels, "Murder on the Orient Express" is a masterpiece of plot construction and psychological insight. Set aboard the luxurious train traveling from Istanbul to Calais, this classic whodunit presents one of literature's most ingenious solutions to an impossible crime.

With its confined setting, diverse cast of suspicious characters, and brilliant detective work by Poirot, this novel showcases Christie's unparalleled ability to mislead and surprise readers until the very last page.
